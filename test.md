## Impression

This examination demonstrates several urgent device-related findings requiring immediate clinical attention: the NG/OG tube is malpositioned within the airway or lung and requires urgent repositioning; the CVC/PICC tip lies within the right atrium and should be withdrawn to the distal SVC; and the right ventricular pacemaker lead appears displaced. Free air is identified beneath the right hemidiaphragm, which in the appropriate clinical context is a critical finding. The cardiomediastinal configuration shows rightward mediastinal shift with obliteration of the aortopulmonary window and right superior mediastinal widening, findings which may represent right-sided volume loss or an extrinsic compressive process. Pulmonary vasculature demonstrates enlarged central arteries with peripheral pruning alongside an interstitial oedema pattern, findings consistent with elevated pulmonary vascular pressures. Bilateral cardiophrenic angle blunting, bilateral diaphragmatic flattening, and left-sided focal hyperlucency with a bulla are additionally noted. A sclerotic vertebral lesion is identified and warrants clinical correlation. An abnormal upper abdominal gas pattern and a large hiatal hernia are present. Valve prosthesis abnormality is noted on a background of prior sternotomy.

## Differential Considerations

- Urgent malpositioned NG/OG tube — airway placement requiring immediate withdrawal
- CVC tip in right atrium — may represent migration from an initially optimal position; repositioning to distal SVC indicated
- Enlarged central pulmonary arteries with peripheral pruning and interstitial oedema may represent pulmonary arterial hypertension with superimposed cardiac decompensation
- Free sub-diaphragmatic air may represent visceral perforation or post-procedural pneumoperitoneum; clinical and surgical correlation required
